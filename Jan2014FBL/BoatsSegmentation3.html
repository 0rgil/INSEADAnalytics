Business Decisions

CreeqBoat needs to expand its business to North America; to support this expansion strategy the Customer Insight department was tasked with conducting market research to gain a better understanding of the North American consumers. In particular, this research will help identify the appropriate customer segments that CreeqBoat should target in order to gain market share and generate increased revenue.

Following the previous factor analysis, the team now proceeds with clustering and segmenting the target clients.

The Data

Market research was conducted through interviews with 3000 boat owners and intenders. Data was collected on customers attitudes toward boating, their demographics, the types of boats owned, use of boats, price of boats, etc. Segmentation of the customers was based on 29 questions about attitudes, needs and motivations about boating.

Responses for these questions range from 1 to 5, with 1 being strongly disagree and 5 being strongly agree.

Our Approach

Clustering and Segmentation in 9 steps

  1. Confirm the data in metric

  2. Decide whether to scale or standardize the data

  3. Decide which variables to use for clustering

  4. Define similarity or dissimilarity measures between observations

  5. Visualize Individual Attributes and Pair-wise Distances between the Observations

  6. Select the clustering method to use and decide how many clusters to have

  7. Profile and interpret the clusters

  8. Assess the robustness of our clusters

Let's follow these steps.

Step 4. Define similarity or dissimilarity measures between observations

In our case we explore two distance metrics: the commonly used Euclidean distance as well as a simple one we define manually.

The Euclidean distance between two observations (in our case, customers) is simply the square root of the average of the square difference between the attributes of the two observations (in our case, customers). Let's now define our own distance metric, as an example. Let's say that the management team of the company believes that two customers are similar if they do not differ in their ratings of the attitudinal questions by more than 2 points. We can manually assign a distance of 1 for every question for which two customers gave an answer that differs by more than 2 points, and 0 otherwise. It is easy to write this distance function in R:

My_Distance_function <- function(x, y) {
    sum(abs(x - y) > 2)
}

Step 6. Select the clustering method to use and decide how many clusters to have

With the available data, the team used the Kmean Clustering Method, as the team have defined 5 key segments to target.

The Dendogram below shows 5 major clusters around its branches.

In this note we will use two widely used methods: the Kmeans Clustering Method, and the Hierarchical Clustering Method. Like all clustering methods, these two also require that we have decided how to measure the distance/similarity between our observations. Explaining how these methods work is beyond our scope. The only difference to highlight is that Kmeans requires the user to define how many segments to create, while Hierarchical Clustering does not.

Let's fist use the Hierarchial Clustering method, as we do not know for now how many segments there are in our data. Hierarchical clustering is a method that also helps us visualise how the data may be clustering together. It generates a plot called the Dendrogram which is often helpful for visualization - but should be used with care. Note that we can draw as many clusters are we choose (e.g. in this case we chose 5 clusters) around the branches of the Dendrogram.

We can also plot the “distances” traveled before we need to merge any of the lower and smaller in size clusters into larger ones - the heights of the tree branches that link the clusters as we traverse the tree from its leaves to its root. If we have n observations, this plot has n-1 numbers.

INTERPRETATION

Reviewing, the segments could be defined as follows. Do note that the marital status was not used in this analysis as it did not provide a clear scale in determining its significance in the profiling:

SOCIAL BUTTERFLY She is most likely a lady who enjoys boating with other people. She also views boating as a social event (Q7, 11, & 9-5). However due to her limited knowledge of the brand, she most likely will not make recommendations on which boat brands to purchase (Q17).

Lastly this segment falls closer to the $74,000 to $84,999 income bracket (Q12).

PRACTICAL USER This is a mixed group with more women than men, and brand image is of high importance (Q11). They view boating as social events, and likely to be self-employed or temporarily not working (Q7 & 14).

This segment is at the intermediate boating experience level, and are likely owners of a powerboat, but they only require a lower range horsepower boat if they were to purchase one (Q2, 5 & 8). Overall this group does not view brand and superior style as highly important (Q16). However due to their limited knowledge of the brand, they most likely will not make recommendations on which boat brands to purchase (Q17).

Lastly this segment falls closer to the $74,000 to $84,999 income bracket (Q12).

AVERAGE JOE This is a group composed of mostly men who neither have a distinct fascination nor a stark dislike for boating (Q11). Almost everyone in this group currently own a powerboat however, relative to all other segments, they have the lowest purchase price for their current boats (Q2 & 4). They do not have strong attitudes towards the sport nor do they have a specific use for their boats (Q16).

This segment can be further studied because it currently has no clear preference. Due to their lack of fascination for boating in general, this group will most likely not recommend the purchase of a CreeqBaots product. The objective then is to be able to influence them into recognizing the brand and gearing them away from being passive boaters.

Lastly this segment falls closer to the $60,000 to $74,999 income bracket (Q12).

BRAND ADDICTS This segment believes brand image is of high importance, which means leadership in cutting edge technology, innovations and a strong customer service offering (Q 16). They spend over 45 days (approximately 2 months) boating a year, and likely to be self-employed (Q10 & 14).

This segment is at the intermediate/advanced boating experience level, and they only require a mid-range horsepower boat if they were to purchase one (Q5 & 8). However due to their limited knowledge of the brand, they most likely will not make recommendations on which boat brands to purchase (Q17 & 18). Yet to be high in the mind of this segment, CreeqBoats will need to create a brand that is high on awareness and be able to impress others to give this segment the edge it is seeking.

Lastly this segment falls closer to the $74,000 to $84,999 income bracket (Q12).

TECHIE NEWBIE This is a segment that values the power that a boat has and prefers to have at least a mid-range horsepower boat (Q8). Having intermediate experience in boating, they are more knowledgeable about boat specifications (Q5).

Relative to others, this is a fairly young cluster of boaters (Q15). Given this, it is expected for them to use their boat for socializing, however, data suggests the otherwise and shows that they occasionally boat alone. (Q7).

Lastly this segment falls closer to the $50,000 to $59,999 income bracket (Q12).

Interpretation, Decisions and Conclusion

In our view, Creeqboat could use marketing campaigns to target each cluster separately, focusing on the main characteristic to call the attention.

As far as the number of segments, the team concluded that this is the maximum Creeqboat should allocate marketing resources towards. While CreeqBoat should also keep in mind the potential for further segregation the segments when the CreeqBoat brand reach matrurity from a brand awareness perspective.
