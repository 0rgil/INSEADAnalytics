To define the growth strategy for CreeqBoat, we need to understand the Customer segments. The Data

Data from consumer survey includes:

  • 29 attitudes towards boating rated on 5 point scale (used for factor analysis)
  • 18 survey questions to understand purchase power and behavior (will be included for cluster analysis) From 3000 boat owners/intenders (consumers)

Our Approach

Factor Analysis in 6 steps

  1. Confirm the data in metric

  2. Decide whether to scale or standardize the data

  3. Check the correlation matrix to see if Factor Analysis makes sense

  4. Develop a scree plot and decide on the number of factors to be derived

  5. Interpret the factors (consider factor rotations - technical but useful)

  6. Save factor scores for subsequent analyses

Step 4. Develop a scree plot and decide on the number of factors to be derived

At this step, we will plot all the 29 attributes on a scree plot and evaluate the number of components used on the basis of following criteria: 1. Eigenvalue > 1 2. Cumulative variance explained 3. “Elbow” in the Scree plot




Although the 'eigenvalue' criteria suggets us to use 5 components, through multiple iterations upto 'cluster analysis' we selected 13 components at this stage. This ensured 5 significant clusters at the next stage. Moreover, 13 components explained 74% of variance of data which makes it much more representative of the entire data.

Intepretation, Decisions and Conclusion

To summarize, we have tried to answer the following questions through the analysis above:

  • How many factors should we use? Why? Quantitative and Qualitative criteria We intend to use 13 factors that explain ~74% of the variance of initial survey. While we use standard criteria of eigenvalue, variance explained and elbow on scree curve to define number of factors to be used, we further did iterations to ensure we get significant 5 clusters in the next step of cluster analysis. That made us select 13 factors at this stage of the analysis.

  • How can we name and interpret the factors? Basis the loading on individual factors, we defined the overarching attribute described by the new derived factor and named them accordingly. Details of the same are in Section 5 of the report.

  • What are some issues to consider? There are several aspects/issues with the current analysis that we need to continue to consider:

a) Current selection of factors is decided based on a cluster analysis. As we move beyond cluster analysis to
segmentation, we may find discrepancies and may have to come back to factor analysis to redefine number of factors to be used.

b) The current selection only explains 74% of the variance of the original data set and hence already ignores certain aspects of complete data

c) Since the incremental variance explained beyond 5 factors is minimal, we may be increasing complexity of analysis by adding more factors
